FAQ

Common questions

Does ShahoSuite produce VAT-compliant invoices for UK businesses?
Yes. Every invoice includes all HMRC-required fields — VAT registration number, unique invoice number, net and VAT amounts shown separately, supplier and customer address. Compliant by default for UK VAT-registered businesses.
What invoice statuses does ShahoSuite support?
ShahoSuite supports six statuses: Draft (created, not sent), Sent (emailed to customer), Partially Paid (part payment received), Paid (fully settled), Overdue (past due date — auto-applied), and Cancelled (voided). Status is selected on save and can be updated any time from the invoice list.
How do credit notes work?
Credit notes are linked directly to the original invoice. Issue a full or partial credit note in one click — accounting records and outstanding balances update automatically. No separate spreadsheets needed.
Does ShahoSuite send automatic payment reminders?
Yes. Reminders are sent automatically based on your credit terms — before the due date, on the due date, and when an invoice becomes overdue. You set the rules once; ShahoSuite handles the chasing.
Is ShahoSuite safe? Where is my data stored?
ShahoSuite is hosted on Microsoft Azure — the same cloud platform used by 95% of Fortune 500 companies. Your data is stored in UK data centres, SSL encrypted, and fully GDPR-compliant. You always own your data.
How much does ShahoSuite cost?
£50/month for the Director account — full access to all 16 modules including invoicing, credit notes, accounting, bookings and more. No per-invoice fees. Staff accounts are £15/month each. See full pricing.
